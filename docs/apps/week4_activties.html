Activity 1: IDE Submission Component Matching

Time: 10 minutes | Match each component with its correct description.

Debrief Questions

  • "Which of these components would be MISSING if you submitted an incomplete IDE application? What would happen?"

    Answer: If any required component missing, FDA issues deficiency letter (21 CFR 812.20(c)). Sponsor has 30 calendar days to respond or application can be deemed disapproved.

  • "For an abbreviated IDE (NSR device), are ALL these components still required?"

    Answer: YES. The abbreviation refers to the APPROVAL PROCESS (IRB-only), not the documentation requirements. NSR investigations must still include all components.

  • "Which component is most critical for protecting subject safety?"

    Answer: Informed Consent Forms—subjects must understand device-specific risks based on prior data before participation.

Activity 2: UADE Reporting Timeline Calculation

Time: 12 minutes | Calculate UADE reporting deadlines. Remember: 10 WORKING DAYS (M-F, exclude federal holidays).

SCENARIO 1: Basic Timeline

Event: Serious device-related infection observed. Investigator Learns: Tuesday, January 16. When must investigator report?

Correct Answer: Tuesday, January 30

Count: Wed 17, Thu 18, Fri 19, Mon 22, Tue 23, Wed 24, Thu 25, Fri 26, Mon 29, Tue 30. (CFR: 21 CFR 812.150(a)(1))

SCENARIO 2: Multi-Week Delay

Event: UADE occurs Monday, March 11. Investigator on vacation, learns Friday, March 22. When is investigator's reporting deadline?

Correct Answer: Friday, April 5

Clock starts from when investigator FIRST LEARNS (Mar 22). Count: Mon 25, Tue 26, Wed 27, Thu 28, Fri 29, Mon Apr 1, Tue 2, Wed 3, Thu 4, Fri 5.

SCENARIO 3: Sponsor's Unreasonable Risk Determination

Sponsor receives UADE notice Wednesday, April 10. By Friday, April 12, determines unreasonable risk. By when must investigation be terminated?

Correct Answer: Friday, April 24

Deadline is the LATER of: 5 working days after determination (Apr 19) OR 15 working days after first notice (Apr 24). The later date is Apr 24. (CFR: 21 CFR 812.46(b)(2))

SCENARIO 5: CHALLENGE—Multiple Staggered Events

Event A: Investigator learns UADE on Friday, Jan 10. Event B: Learns different UADE on Monday, Jan 13. When must each be reported?

Event A Deadline: Friday, January 24 (10 working days from Jan 10)

Event B Deadline: Monday, January 27 (10 working days from Jan 13)

Debrief Questions

  • "Why does the regulation use WORKING DAYS for devices but CALENDAR DAYS for drugs?"

    Answer: Device failures can be mechanical and urgent; working-day timeline ensures reports within business cycle. For drugs, calendar days gives longer time but still ensures rapid reporting.

  • "What happens if investigator misses the 10-day deadline?"

    Answer: Investigator violates 21 CFR 812.150(a)(1). FDA could issue warning letter, note at inspection, or recommend investigator suspension from future IDE work.

Discussion Questions

  • "Why is device accountability a SHARED responsibility...?"

    Answer: Sponsor ships device and maintains records (812.140(b)(4)-(5)), but investigator must prevent diversion and account for each unit (812.110). Both parties have accountability for device control.

  • "Which SPONSOR responsibility would be IMPOSSIBLE to delegate to a CRO?"

    Answer: UADE evaluation and termination decision (812.46(b)). While CRO can manage operations, only sponsor has regulatory authority to determine if risk is unreasonable and terminate.

  • "For UADE reporting, why must BOTH investigator AND sponsor report separately?"

    Answer: Investigator reports to sponsor + IRB per 812.150(a)(1). Sponsor independently reports to FDA + all IRBs + all investigators per 812.150(b)(1). This ensures all parties are informed.

Activity 4: IDE Investigator Agreement Walkthrough

Time: 5 minutes | Review the required elements and take the quick quiz.

Required Investigator Agreement Sections (Review)

SECTION 1: Investigator Identification

Includes: Investigator name, credentials, institution, contact info. (CFR: 812.43(c))

SECTION 2: Relevant Experience Statement

Includes: Dates, location, extent, and type of experience. (CFR: 812.43(c)(1))

SECTION 3: Prior Termination Explanation

Includes: Explanation of circumstances IF terminated, or statement of no prior terminations. (CFR: 812.43(c)(2))

SECTION 4: Investigator Commitments

Agrees to: Conduct investigation per plan/regulations, supervise testing, ensure informed consent, report UADEs, etc. (CFR: 812.43(c)(3)-(6))

SECTION 5: Financial Disclosure Certification

Includes: Certification of providing financial info, commitment to update. (CFR: 812.110)

SECTION 6: Signatures & Dates

Includes: Investigator signature and date, sponsor/CRO signature and date. (CFR: 812.43(c))

Activity Debrief: Final Discussion

  • "What's the difference between an Investigator Agreement and an Informed Consent Form?"

    Answer: Agreement = regulatory commitment from investigator to sponsor (21 CFR 812.43(c)). ICF = disclosure to subjects about device risks/study procedures (21 CFR 50).

  • "If an investigator violates the signed agreement, can the sponsor just call them...?"

    Answer: Sponsor can try to "secure compliance" (corrective action plan), but if investigator won't comply, sponsor MUST discontinue device shipments and terminate participation per 21 CFR 812.46(a).
