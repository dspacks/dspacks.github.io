Overview

Calendar awareness at a glance

Sectograph is a watchface built for people who live in meetings, appointments, shifts, and structured days. It represents the day as a circular 24-hour dial and renders calendar events as colored arcs, so you can see time blocks, overlaps, and how the rest of the day flows — all without opening your phone.

The design is intentionally simple: the dial is the story. Event sectors, a current-time hand, and legible date/battery complications keep the watchface readable at a glance while still conveying real schedule density.

Features

What ships in the current build

  • 24-hour circular dial for an at-a-glance view of the day
  • Colored event sectors to show timed calendar blocks
  • All-day event ring along the outer edge
  • Current-time hand for instant orientation
  • Color themes and settings via Clay
  • Support for five Pebble hardware targets: Emery, Basalt, Chalk, Diorite, and Aplite
Status notes: The core watchface is in private beta. Calendar sync via ICS, weather, rain shading, and companion browsing features are in progress and may be rolled out as future updates.

Why it matters

A productivity tool for structured days

Sectograph is the first product in my app-store lineup because it sits at the intersection of design, systems thinking, and utility. It’s a practical, visual way to understand the day — a good fit for people who want their watch to surface information without demanding attention.

More products will follow this same pattern: lightweight, useful, and built with an LLM-assisted workflow that keeps shipping fast while still preserving quality and iteration.
