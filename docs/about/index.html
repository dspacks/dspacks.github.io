About Me

Strategic Operations and Data Team Manager — sitting at the intersection of research, public health, population health, implementation, and informatics

Who I Am

I'm David Spatholt — Strategic Operations and Data Team Manager in the Department of Emergency Medicine at The Ohio State University Wexner Medical Center. I direct budgeting, grant management, data operations, and systems development for a portfolio of NIH-funded clinical research — leading a team that spans data analysis, research informatics, and program administration.

My official HR title is Clinical Research Manager, but my actual scope is the strategic operations and data infrastructure that makes a multi-million-dollar research program run.

My career spans 13+ years across government, academic, and clinical research environments — from managing HUD programs and CDBG grants at the county level, to leading EDA revolving loan funds, to directing clinical research operations in emergency medicine. The thread running through it all: program management, systems thinking, data operations, and building tools that make complex work manageable.

Where I Sit

I work at a deliberate crossroads — the intersection of clinical research, public health, population health, health services and implementation research, and informatics. Most people live in one of these domains. I build the bridges between them.

In practice, that means I lead the data systems and operational strategy that let a public health screening program (HealthNow) run smoothly inside an emergency department, while simultaneously supporting health services research on opioid decision-making and trauma recovery, while also designing the informatics infrastructure — REDCap databases, SQL queries, code-sharing via git, dashboards — that makes all of it measurable, reproducible, and improvable.

I don't just manage one piece. I sit where they all connect — and I build the systems that keep them working together.

Cross-Domain Competencies

  • Clinical research operations: Budgeting, grants compliance, data architecture, team leadership for NIH-funded studies
  • Public & population health: ED-based screening programs, SUD/HIV/HCV prevention, linkage-to-care operations
  • Health services & implementation research: Study support from IRB through publication — data systems, grant logistics, measurement
  • Informatics: REDCap, SQL, Python/R, Power BI, git — the infrastructure that makes research reproducible
  • Systems thinking & tool building: Dashboards, schedulers, automation — tools that make complex work manageable

How I Work

I approach problems from a systems perspective — understanding how pieces connect before deciding what to build. Whether it's designing a grant tracking system, optimizing a clinical research workflow, or coding a nurse scheduling algorithm with OR-Tools, I start with the structure of the problem and work toward the simplest solution that actually works.

I'm a heavy user of data and automation. Python, R, SQL, REDCap, Power BI, git, and increasingly LLMs are the tools I reach for. I believe the best systems are the ones that make the right thing easy and the wrong thing hard.
