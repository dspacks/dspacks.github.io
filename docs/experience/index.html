Experience

13+ years across government, academic, and clinical research settings

Career Arc

My career started in community and economic development — managing HUD/CDBG programs, EDA revolving loan funds, and municipal planning projects across northern Kentucky. That foundation in program management, compliance, and systems thinking carries through everything I do today.

In 2020, I pivoted to clinical research at the University of Cincinnati, leading COVID testing operations and deploying data systems for SUD/HIV screening programs. I advanced from senior research professional to data team manager, building the operational infrastructure for a portfolio of public health research.

Today I direct Strategic Operations and Data Team Management at Ohio State's Department of Emergency Medicine — overseeing grant management, data infrastructure, and the systems that support NIH-funded research on pain, addiction, trauma, and health equity. I lead a team that spans data analysis, research informatics, and program administration, directing both the strategy and the day-to-day operations of a multi-million-dollar research program.

Across every role, the pattern is the same: build the system, connect the data, make the operation run. Whether it's a loan fund portfolio, a COVID testing pipeline, or a clinical trial — I lead through structure, measurement, and tools.

What makes my work distinctive is where I sit: at the intersection of research, public health, population health, health services and implementation research, and informatics. I don't just work in one domain — I build the data systems, operational infrastructure, and strategic frameworks that connect them all.
